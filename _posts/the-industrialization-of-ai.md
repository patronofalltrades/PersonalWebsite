The most important thing I've learned is conviction: the ability to see an idea clearly and refuse to let it die until it becomes real. That's what separates operators from observers.

I believe Southeast Asia is on the verge of something significant. In the next decade, as AI supercharges productivity across industries, the region won't just be a participant in the global economy. It will be a proving ground for how emerging economies leapfrog into the future. I want to help initiate that.

### 1. The Physical World Needs Software

When we began building our VR edtech platform, Asperio, the thesis was simple: the physical world needs software to scale training. What we found was that the barrier wasn't technology; it was the lack of localized, practical implementations that factories could actually use without massive overhead.

AI changes this equation entirely.

### 2. General-Purpose Robotics

General-purpose robotics and vision models are no longer constrained to highly controlled environments. We are moving from hard-coded automation (where a robotic arm does one precise thing 10,000 times) to adaptive automation (where the system observes, learns, and reacts to anomalies).
